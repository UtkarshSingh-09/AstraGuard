HRA Exemption Calculation Rules

The HRA (House Rent Allowance) exemption is the MINIMUM of three conditions:

1. Actual HRA received from employer
2. Rent paid minus 10% of basic salary
3. 50% of basic salary (metro cities: Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, Chennai) 
   OR 40% of basic salary (non-metro cities)

IMPORTANT NOTES:
- If rent paid is ZERO, HRA exemption is ZERO (regardless of HRA received)
- HRA exemption is available ONLY under OLD regime
- Must have actual rent receipts or rental agreement as proof
- Landlord PAN is mandatory if annual rent exceeds ₹1,00,000
- Cannot claim both HRA exemption AND home loan interest Section 24(b) for the SAME city
  (can claim both if HRA for rented house in work city and home loan for house in different city)

COMMON MISTAKE:
- Many employees receive HRA but don't pay rent → exemption = ₹0
- Some claim full HRA as exemption → WRONG, must take minimum of 3 conditions

Section 24(b) — Home Loan Interest:
- Maximum deduction: ₹2,00,000 per year for self-occupied property
- Available under OLD regime only
- For let-out property: full interest deductible (no limit)
- Both co-owners can claim ₹2L each on joint home loan
