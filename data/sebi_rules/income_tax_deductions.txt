Section 80CCD(1B) — NPS Additional Deduction

1. DEDUCTION LIMIT: ₹50,000 per year (OVER AND ABOVE 80C limit)
2. ONLY available in OLD regime
3. Combined with 80CCD(1), total NPS deduction cannot exceed 10% of salary for salaried individuals
4. But 80CCD(1B) is a SEPARATE ₹50,000 deduction — additional to 80C's ₹1.5L
5. Total tax-saving potential: ₹1.5L (80C) + ₹50K (80CCD1B) = ₹2L deduction
6. At 30% slab: ₹50,000 deduction saves ₹15,600 tax (including cess)
7. NPS Tier 1 has partial lock-in until age 60
8. 60% of NPS corpus can be withdrawn tax-free at maturity
9. 40% must be used to buy annuity

Section 80D — Health Insurance Deduction

1. SELF/FAMILY: Up to ₹25,000 (₹50,000 if senior citizen)
2. PARENTS: Additional ₹25,000 (₹50,000 if parents are senior citizens)
3. Maximum total: ₹1,00,000 (self senior + parents senior)
4. Preventive health check-up: ₹5,000 (within the overall limit)
5. ONLY available in OLD regime
6. Covers: health insurance premium, preventive health checkup
7. Does NOT cover: life insurance premium (that's 80C)

Section 80TTA — Savings Account Interest

1. Deduction up to ₹10,000 on savings account interest
2. ONLY for savings accounts (not FD, RD)
3. Available in OLD regime only
4. For senior citizens: Section 80TTB allows ₹50,000 on all deposits
