SEBI (Mutual Funds) Regulations — Key Rules for AstraGuard

1. EXPENSE RATIO LIMITS (SEBI/HO/IMD/DF2/CIR/P/2018/137):
- Maximum Total Expense Ratio (TER) for equity schemes: varies by AUM slab
- First ₹500 Cr: max 2.25% (Regular), 1.25% less for Direct
- Direct plans must have lower expense ratios than Regular plans
- Exit load: max 2%, typically 1% for redemption within 1 year

2. DIRECT vs REGULAR PLANS:
- Direct plans do not pay distributor commissions
- Expense ratio difference typically 0.5% to 1.5%
- Over 10 years, this compounds significantly
- Example: ₹10L invested, 1% expense difference = ₹1.6L+ loss over 10 years
- SEBI mandates disclosure of both plan options

3. INVESTOR PROTECTION:
- AMCs must prominently display "Mutual fund investments are subject to market risks"
- No guaranteed returns can be promised
- Past performance is not indicative of future results
- Risk-o-meter must be displayed for each scheme

4. KYC REQUIREMENTS:
- Mandatory KYC verification for investments above ₹50,000
- PAN card mandatory for all mutual fund investments
- SEBI-registered KRAs (KYC Registration Agencies) manage verification

5. SIP REGULATIONS:
- Minimum SIP amount varies by AMC (typically ₹500-₹1000)
- SIP can be paused (not cancelled) in most schemes
- No penalty for SIP pause, but lock-in applies for ELSS (3 years)
