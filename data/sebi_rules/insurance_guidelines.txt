Term Insurance — Adequacy Guidelines

1. COVER CALCULATION (HUMAN LIFE VALUE METHOD):
- Cover needed = Annual income × Years to retirement × Income replacement factor
- Simple formula: Cover = Annual salary × (retirement age - current age) × 0.7
- Alternatively: 10-15× annual income as thumb rule
- Must consider: outstanding loans, dependents' future expenses, children's education

2. ADDITIONAL FACTORS:
- Outstanding home loan amount → add to cover
- Children's education corpus → add ₹50L-₹1Cr per child
- Spouse dependency → if non-earning, factor living expenses until their life expectancy
- Existing group insurance from employer → subtract from needed cover
- Other life insurance policies → subtract from needed cover

3. PREMIUM GUIDELINES:
- Buy ONLINE term plan (cheapest, direct from insurer)
- Premium typically 0.2-0.5% of cover for age 25-35
- Example: ₹1 Cr cover at age 30 ≈ ₹8,000-₹12,000/year
- Premium increases sharply after age 40 — buy early

4. IMPORTANT NOTES:
- ONLY term insurance recommended for cover — NOT ULIP, endowment, money-back
- ULIPs and endowment plans are investment + insurance hybrid → poor at both
- Term plan = pure protection at lowest cost
- Riders: Critical illness rider worth considering, accident rider optional
- Must disclose: We are not insurance advisors, consult IRDAI-registered agent

5. TAX BENEFIT:
- Premium paid qualifies under Section 80C (up to ₹1.5L combined limit)
- Death benefit is tax-free under Section 10(10D) for nominee
