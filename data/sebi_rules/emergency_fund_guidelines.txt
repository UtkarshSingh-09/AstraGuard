Emergency Fund Guidelines — Best Practices

1. STANDARD RECOMMENDATION: 6 months of monthly expenses
- Minimum: 3 months for dual-income households
- Maximum: 12 months for single-income with dependents
- For freelancers/self-employed: 9-12 months recommended

2. CALCULATION:
- Monthly expenses (rent + groceries + EMIs + insurance + utilities + discretionary)
- Emergency fund = Monthly expenses × 6
- Example: ₹80,000/month expenses → Emergency fund = ₹4,80,000

3. WHERE TO KEEP:
- Liquid mutual funds (instant redemption up to ₹50,000)
- Savings account (instant access, but low returns ~3-4%)
- FD with premature withdrawal facility (avoid lock-in FDs)
- Split: 50% liquid fund + 50% savings account for optimal accessibility

4. IMPORTANT NOTES:
- Emergency fund is NOT an investment — it's insurance against unexpected events
- Do NOT invest emergency fund in equity, ELSS, or long-term instruments
- Review and top-up annually as expenses increase
- Medical insurance reduces emergency fund needs (if comprehensive)

5. WHAT COUNTS AS EMERGENCY:
- Job loss or income disruption
- Medical emergency (beyond insurance coverage)
- Urgent home/vehicle repairs
- Family emergencies

6. WHAT DOES NOT COUNT:
- Planned expenses (vacation, gadgets, wedding)
- Investment opportunities ("market is cheap")
- Down payment for assets
