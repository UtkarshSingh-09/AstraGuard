SEBI — Return Claims and Assumptions Guidelines

1. PROHIBITED CLAIMS:
- "Guaranteed returns" — strictly prohibited
- "Assured returns" — prohibited  
- "Risk-free investment" — prohibited
- "Sure shot profit" — prohibited
- "Double your money" — prohibited unless government scheme with fixed maturity
- "Zero risk" — no investment is zero risk

2. REASONABLE RETURN ASSUMPTIONS:
- Historical Nifty50 CAGR: approximately 12-13% over 20+ years
- Conservative equity assumption: 10-12% CAGR
- Moderate equity assumption: 12% CAGR  
- Aggressive equity assumption: 13% CAGR (should not exceed this)
- Using >13% equity return = UNREALISTIC and potentially misleading
- Debt returns: 6-7.5% range is reasonable
- Using >8% debt return = questionable

3. INFLATION ASSUMPTIONS:
- Historical CPI inflation India: 5-7% average
- Reasonable range: 5-7% for planning
- Conservative: 6% is widely used standard
- Using <4% inflation = potentially misleading (too optimistic)
- Using >8% inflation = potentially misleading (too pessimistic)

4. SAFE WITHDRAWAL RATE:
- The "4% rule" is a guideline, not a guarantee
- India-specific: some experts recommend 3-3.5% due to higher inflation
- Must be presented as a guideline with disclaimer
- Example disclaimer: "Based on historical data. Actual results may vary."

5. SEBI MANDATED DISCLAIMERS:
- "Mutual fund investments are subject to market risks. Read all scheme related documents carefully."
- "Past performance is not indicative of future results."
- Must be displayed prominently, not buried in fine print
