Capital Gains Tax — LTCG and STCG Rules (FY 2026-27)

1. EQUITY MUTUAL FUNDS & STOCKS:

SHORT-TERM CAPITAL GAINS (STCG):
- Holding period: Less than 12 months (1 year)
- Tax rate: 20% (increased from 15% in Budget 2024)
- No exemption limit for STCG
- Applicable on: Equity MF, stocks, equity-oriented hybrid funds

LONG-TERM CAPITAL GAINS (LTCG):
- Holding period: 12 months or more
- Tax rate: 12.5% (increased from 10% in Budget 2024)
- Exemption: First ₹1,25,000 of LTCG per year is TAX-FREE
- Applicable on: Equity MF, stocks listed on recognized exchange
- Indexation benefit removed from Budget 2024

2. DEBT MUTUAL FUNDS:
- All gains taxed as per income tax slab (no special rate)
- No distinction between short-term and long-term
- This changed from April 2023 — earlier had indexation benefit

3. REBALANCING TAX IMPLICATIONS:
- Selling equity MF within 1 year = 20% STCG tax on gains
- Selling equity MF after 1 year = 12.5% LTCG (above ₹1.25L exemption)
- ALWAYS check holding period before recommending fund exits
- If fund exit triggers STCG, recommend waiting for LTCG threshold

4. TAX HARVESTING:
- Can book ₹1.25L LTCG each year tax-free
- Reinvest immediately in same or different fund
- Effective way to reduce future tax liability
- STCG harvesting rarely beneficial due to 20% rate
