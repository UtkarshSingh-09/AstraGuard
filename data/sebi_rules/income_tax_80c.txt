Income Tax Act — Section 80C Deductions (FY 2026-27)

1. MAXIMUM DEDUCTION: ₹1,50,000 per financial year (OLD REGIME ONLY)

2. ELIGIBLE INSTRUMENTS:
- ELSS Mutual Funds: Lock-in 3 years, equity-linked, potential 12-15% returns
- PPF (Public Provident Fund): Lock-in 15 years, current rate ~7.1%, tax-free returns
- EPF (Employee Provident Fund): Employer + Employee contribution
- NSC (National Savings Certificate): 5-year lock-in
- Tax-saving FD: 5-year lock-in, ~6.5-7% returns
- Life Insurance Premium: Deduction limited to 10% of sum assured
- Home Loan Principal Repayment: Part of EMI that goes to principal
- Tuition Fees: For up to 2 children, actual fees paid
- Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana: For girl child, current rate ~8%

3. IMPORTANT NOTES:
- 80C deduction is NOT available under New Tax Regime (except EPF employer contribution)
- Total limit of ₹1,50,000 is COMBINED across all 80C instruments
- Don't exceed ₹1,50,000 — no benefit beyond this limit
- ELSS has shortest lock-in (3 years) among 80C options
- PPF interest is completely tax-free (EEE — Exempt Exempt Exempt)

4. COMMON MISTAKES:
- Counting employer EPF contribution: Only employee contribution qualifies
- Exceeding ₹1,50,000: No additional benefit
- Not considering lock-in period: Choose instruments based on your liquidity needs
